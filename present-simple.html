Present Simple

The ultimate guide. Learn how to build it, spelling rules for verbs, marker words, and all 7 use cases.

How it looks on a timeline:

Past
✖✖✖Now✖✖✖
Future

Actions repeat regularly: yesterday, today, and tomorrow.

1. Structure

✓ Affirmative

I / You / We / They + Verb

He / She / It + Verb-s/es

👉 I work in an office.

👉 She works at home.

✗ Negative

I / You / We / They + do not + Verb

He / She / It + does not + Verb

👉 They don't like loud music.

👉 He doesn't drive a car.

? Question

Do + I / you / we / they + Verb?

Does + he / she / it + Verb?

👉 Do you speak English?

👉 Does it rain often here?

💡 Short Forms

do not ➔ don't

does not ➔ doesn't

2. Spelling Rules (He/She/It)

Rule Example
Most verbs: add -s work ➔ works
play ➔ plays
Ends in -ch, -sh, -s, -x, -o: add -es watch ➔ watches
go ➔ goes
Consonant + y: change to -ies study ➔ studies
cry ➔ cries

3. Time Markers

Words that hint you should use Present Simple. They usually go before the main verb.

always
usually
often
sometimes
rarely
never
every day/week

Where to put them?

  • Before normal verbs: I always wake up early.
  • After the verb 'to be' (am/is/are): She is never late.
  • At the end (for phrases like 'every day'): We play tennis every Sunday.

⚠️ The 'to be' Exception

The verb 'to be' (am/is/are) doesn't need 'do' or 'does' for negatives and questions.

I don't be a doctor. ➔ I am not a doctor.

4. All 7 Use Cases Explained

1. Habits & Routines

Things you do regularly.

I usually drink coffee in the morning.

2. General Facts & Truths

Things that are always true or facts of nature.

Water boils at 100°C.

3. Timetables & Schedules

Used for fixed future events (trains, flights, movies).

The train leaves at 8:00 PM tonight.

4. State Verbs (Non-action)

Verbs of feelings and thoughts (love, know, need) don't use -ing.

I need help right now.

5. Instructions & Directions

When explaining how to do something.

First, you crack the eggs. Then, you mix them.

6. Time & Conditional Clauses (Future)

After 'if', 'when', 'until', 'as soon as'.

I will call you when I arrive.

7. Newspaper Headlines & Stories

Used to make past events sound dramatic and current.

Man saves child from fire.

📖 Context Practice: The Farmer's Routine

John is a farmer who lives in a small village. Every morning, his day starts before the sun rises. He wakes up at 5:00 AM because the animals need food. John does not drink coffee; he prefers green tea.

His wife, Sarah, works at the local school. She teaches English to children. Does she help on the farm? Yes, she feeds the chickens every evening. They own several cows and a large field of corn. In this area, the weather changes quickly, but John knows exactly what to do.

On weekends, they do not stay at home. They go to the market to sell their vegetables. People love their organic products because they taste amazing. John believes that hard work brings success. "Why do you work so hard?" his neighbors often ask. He simply smiles and says that he enjoys his life in the countryside.

Vocabulary Focus:

Village – a very small town in the country.
Rise – when the sun comes up in the morning.
Prefer – to like one thing more than another.
Feed – to give food to a person, animal, or plant.

Mastery Test

Let's check all the use cases.

1. My brother never ___ (to make) his bed.

2. Hurry up! The movie ___ (to start) in 10 minutes!

3. She ___ (to study) hard and ___ (to know) the answers.

4. We will go for a walk as soon as the rain ___ (to stop).
